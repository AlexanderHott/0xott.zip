In [Refactoring UI](https://www.refactoringui.com/), one of the greatest design
resources for developers I've ever read, the authors make a very important point
about content:

> Hierarchy is Everything.
>
> Visual hierarchy refers to how important the elements in an interface appear
> in relation to one another.
>
> -- Refactoring UI, 35-36

Any piece of well made textual information has hierarchy. News stories have a
headline and the rest of the article. Textbooks have chapter titles, section
titles, asides, and the main body text. If all text is the same size, reading it
becomes a lot more difficult.

The great thing about the second example is that markdown can transform the
first into the second with just a few extra characters.

```diff
-How to Make Pancakes
+# How to Make Pancakes

-Ingredients
+## Ingredients

 - 1 cup flour
 - 2 eggs
 - 1 cup milk
 - 2 tbsp sugar
 - 2 tsp baking powder
 - 1/2 tsp salt
 - Butter for cooking

-Instructions
+## Instructions

1. Mix Dry Ingredients:
   1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
2. Add Wet Ingredients:
   1. Whisk in eggs and milk until smooth.
3. Cook the Pancakes
   1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat.
   2. Melt a little butter in the pan.
   3. Pour in 1/4 cup of batter per pancake.
   4. Cook until bubbles form, then flip.
   5. Cook until golden on the other side.

-Serving Suggestions
+## Serving Suggestions

Top with:

 - Maple syrup
 - Fresh fruit
 - Whipped cream
```
